About Gender Discount

Lolly, the Italian job market

This website is an infopoetry project created by Sofia Peracchi for the Density Design Lab, at Politecnico di Milano. The project focuses on gender pay gap in Italy, based on the study conducted by Osservatorio Job Pricing, that you can find here. The dataset shows that gender pay gap still occurs, despite the laws that regulate work remuneration: this disparity still affects the Italian job market, severely affecting workers’ lives as well as their relationships among the workplace and the general content they have towards their employment. This artefact wants to shed a light on these disparities, focusing on how the perception of people’s worth is jeopardised because of the differences in their income.

The artefact uses Barbie©, a brand symbol of capitalistic globalisation and inaccurate portrayal of the reality, to represent how real and impacting gender gap really is. The brand is here re-named as “Lolly”, an English word for money, while workers are represented by toys, both female and male. Their monthly salary is indicated by the price of each doll and the “discount” represents the gender pay gap between two people with the same qualification, but of different sexes.

The dataset

The dataset used for this project is extracted from the Gender Gap Report, collected by Osservatiorio Job Pricing in 2020, in collaboration with Spring Professional. This dataset was created to contribute to the debate concerning gender gap, especially in the Italian job market, and to promote gender equality, starting from the workplace. The dataset contains:
  • The number of employed people per category of employment, both in the public and private sector, divided between men and women.
    It shows that men are on average more employed than women in all the categories, except for office workers. Moreover, we can see that in the public sector the gender gap is thinner than in the private sector, in which the disparity is often near 20%.
  • Average salary per sector, for men and women.
    It is possible to detect that the highest gender gap in men’s favour is reported in financial industries and services, while females are facilitated in construction companies.
  • Average salary per category of employment both for men and women.
    We can notice the the higher pay gap lies among workers and office workers, while male and female executives present “only” a 4,4% pay gap.
  • Average salary per industry, for men and women, showing also where more women are employed.
    The dataset shows that the highest gender gap (disadvantageous for women) concerns the industries in which they are the most employed, while the wage disparity in their favour occur in those business where there are less female workers.

The medium

The infopoem uses a navigable and interactive website as a medium to let the user explore and discover the project by himself/herself. Scrolling the page, the user makes decisions, choosing a path, a doll, and later discovering the gap it entangles. By making this choice, the user will be forced to reflect on the topic of gender gap and the deep inequality this leads to in the Western world, where too many people like to think to have already reached equality.

The meaning

The project uses a familiar showcase, typical of online shopping, and a website similar to Barbie©'s, to make the navigation easily understandable. The user's attention is therefore drawn to the elements that are not present in the original web page: the gender discounts and the unreasonably high prices. By visiting the product page, the user’s expectations are answered, and the clear message of the webpage is explained.

Argument and intention

This infopoem aims to make the audience reflect on the reality of gender pay gap through familiar metaphors, such as toys and shopping discounts. Many people do not really realise how wide gender pay gap still is, and that it does not only affect women: this website wants to show the reality of the numbers, by putting a price on people’s work (like salaries actually do) sheding a light on the unfair disparity between workers with the same qualifications. Moreover, the artifact wants to show how gender pay gap is not equal to all sectors, but varies according to the type of industry and the kind of labour requested.
The infopoem only takes in consideration data from Italy, but it would also be interesting to expand the analysis to different European states, showing the differences not only between sectors, but also among different countries.
